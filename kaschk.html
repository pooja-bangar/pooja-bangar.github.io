Kaschk: Mobile Website

Brief

As part of the UX/UI Design part of the Startup Institute Program, I was given the challenge of designing a mobile website for a coffee shop. I chose Kaschk, a recently opened third-wave coffee shop and bar.

Process

User Research

The project was conducted with fellow members of the Product and Design Track. We began by collectively brainstorming the potential needs of customers, and then individually created personas of key coffee shop users. Armed with hypotheses, the next task was to focus on finding out the needs of Kaschk customers. I visited Kaschk on 5 separate occasions with the intention of speaking with a wide range of individuals and observe interactions. I used this information to identify common customer needs and factors that make Kaschk unique.

Prototyping

Having collected extensive user research, I began building a prototype by mapping out each section of the site. I quickly sketched out the first iteration on Post-it notes and uploaded the images to POP to create my first prototype. I carried on with this process, conducting Usability Testing with my cohort members to improve upon each iteration.


Final Design

The final iteration incorporated the following features

  • A home page that displays location details and opening hours
  • A navigation bar that slides in and out when needed so that the main focus of each screen is content about Kaschk
  • A slider button that allows the user to change to day or night mode, depending on when they want to visit
