Personal Project: Zalando app

Brief

Being Europe's largest dedicated online fashion retailer, Zalando really focuses on building a great relationship with their end customer. With a passion for fashion e-commerce and the determination to develop my UX Design skills, I set myself the challenge of exploring ways to support this relationship through the Zalando app.

Process

My journey began by researching Key Trends in Fashion e-commerce and consumer behaviour, and comparing this to what is already in place on the app.

Key Trends

  • Personalisation - allowing consumers to interact with brands on their terms
  • Community - leveraging Social Media to assist consumers in seeking validation to make a purchase
  • Mobile first - creating a minimal but beautiful experience focusing on ease of use and navigation

Already in place

  • Photo search - allowing customers to upload a photo and find similar products
  • Last seen - allowing customers to go straight to products viewed during last login
  • Sharing - giving customers the option to share items via message, email, and social media

My next step was to speak to real users of Fashion Mobile Commerce, as well as explore my own needs as customer, and use this information to identify key pain points and possible solutions.

Farrah - Time-poor mum

31, female, British

Goal

To browse and buy quickly, as she doesn't have much time in between working and looking after her children

Situation

In the evening, using her iPhone as her husband is usually using the laptop

Purchasing Behaviour

Regularly buys one item at a time through browsing. Searches for specfic items every 3-4 months when she has an event to go to.

Purchase validation

Sharing pieces she is thinking of buying in a private group of friends on Facebook

Pain points

Logging back in to her account after being distracted and not be directed to the items she was looking at last

Wishes

More personalised recommendations so that she can find things she likes quickly

Andrea - No-nonsense buyer

38, male, Italian

Goal

To buy exactly what he needs, and not worry about returning it

Situation

Late at night at home, as this is the only time he has to himself

Purchasing Behaviour

Specifically seaches for items that he is looking to buy, doesn't browse

Purchase validation

By looking closely at photos and reviews of garments he's considering purchasing

Pain points

Returning an item because it looks or feels different to what he was expecting

Wishes

More product information and photos so that can he make the right decision.

Pooja - Impulse buyer

38, female, British

Goal

To find exactly what she wants, and have it delivered quickly

Situation

At home in the evening, so that she can make sure potential purchases go with what she already has in her wardrobe

Purchasing Behaviour

Searches for specific items that she may have seen somebody else wearing, either on the street or on a blog such as 'The Sartorialist'

Purchase validation

Buying from brands that she knows she likes and suit her

Pain points

Receiving suggestions that do not relate to her browsing/order history

Wishes

Being able to save preferences, styles and brands she likes so that she doesn't have to search for them every time she logs in

Common Pain Points

  • Content that isn't relevant to what the user is looking for
  • Not having enough control of what items are being recommended
  • Going to other sources to validate purchases
  • Insufficient product information

Proposed Solutions

  • A detailed profile that allows users to input their likes and what they're looking for
  • An event calendar that allows users to enter events and what they need to purchase for that event
  • Functionality that allows users to find friends and create groups that they can share items within
  • Content that is tailored to each customer based on the above features
  • Allowing users to ask questions about items

I decided to structure my insights by creating a mind map of how different areas of the app would be clustered and identifying key features for each section. I sketched out the navigation and flow on Post-it notes and uploaded the sketches to Marvel to make an interactive prototype with which I conducted usability testing.

Usability Testing Feedback

  • Uniform interactions - user naturally gravitated towards clicking on text rather than arrows to make a selection
  • Always having option to see wishlist and bag when shopping
  • Ability to change view of search results, as some items require a more detailed picture than others

Based on the feedback, I created my second prototype using Balsamiq wireframes that were also imported into Marvel. My final design aims to provide a very personalised experience, with relevant recommendations that facilitate a higher conversion rate.

Further Iterations

This is a work in progress so currently I am working on improving the links between each screen, and making it easier for the customer to browse from the home page.
